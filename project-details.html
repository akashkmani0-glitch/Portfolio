Fiber optics is a technology that transmits data, voice, and images as light signals through thin strands of glass or plastic fibers. It offers high-speed communication, large bandwidth, and long-distance data transmission with minimal signal loss. Commonly used in internet networks, telecommunications, and medical imaging, fiber optics provides faster and more reliable connections than traditional copper cables.

The Smart Helmet project is an innovative safety system designed to protect riders and enhance road safety. It typically integrates sensors, microcontrollers, and wireless communication to detect accidents, ensure helmet usage, and monitor vital signs. Some smart helmets include features like alcohol detection, GPS tracking, fall detection, and automatic alert systems to notify emergency contacts. This project aims to reduce road accidents and improve emergency response, promoting safer driving habits.

Visible Light Communication (VLC) is a wireless communication technology that uses visible light (between 400–700 nm) to transmit data. It works by rapidly modulating LED light intensity, which is then received and decoded by a photodiode. VLC offers high-speed data transfer, energy efficiency, and enhanced security since light cannot pass through walls. It is used in smart lighting, indoor navigation, and underwater communication, making it a promising alternative to traditional radio-based systems like Wi-Fi.
